# Embodied Robots

## Fixed-Base Robots

Advantages:

- Robust bases and structures
- Stable and accurate operation
- High programmability

Disadvantages:

- Limited operational range and flexibility
- High cost
- Need for specialized personnel during installation and maintenance

## Wheeled and Tracked Robots

Wheeled robots are suitable for more complex and diverse application scenarios than fixed-base robots.

Advantages of wheeled robots:

- Efficient mobility
- Simple structure
- Low cost
- High energy efficiency
- Rapid movement on flat surfaces

Disadvantage of wheeled robots:

- Limited mobility on complex terrain or harsh, uneven ground

Tracked robots are different from wheeled robots in their terrain adaptability.

Advantages of tracked robots:

- Strong off-road capability
- High maneuverability

Disadvantages of tracked robots:

- Lower speed on flat surfaces
- Lower flexibility and maneuverability compared with wheeled robots

## Quadruped Robots

Quadruped robots are valued for stability and adaptability. They can maintain balance and mobility on uneven surfaces.

Disadvantages:

- High cost
- Limited battery endurance in complex environments

## Humanoid Robots

The integration of LLMs can help humanoid robots handle complex tasks more intelligently.

Advantage:

- Dexterous hand designs enable humanoid robots to perform intricate and complex tasks.

## Biomimetic Robots

Biomimetic robots perform tasks in complex and dynamic environments by simulating the efficient movements and functions of natural organisms.

Advantages:

- Biomimetic designs can improve energy efficiency by mimicking biological movement mechanisms.
- They can make robots more economical in energy consumption.

Disadvantages:

- Complex and costly design
- Limited large-scale production and widespread application
- Durability and reliability still need improvement
